Reliable measurement in environments where short-circuit energy is high

Dangers of Trade - Here are three major risk groups:

a) For voltage measurement: Breakthrough or breakdown in the instrument by overvoltage pulses or abuse (measuring voltage too high). These brief voltage pulses (called transients) are caused by switching operations in the operating medium and low voltage network by motor contactors, by the deletion of the short circuit current protection device. They occur frequently and often reach peak values ​​of several thousand volts. In this case, your safety depends on the dielectric strength of your meter.

Abb. 1.1 a+b: Transients that occur due to switching operations. Typically for short power interruptions are the extreme voltage spikes in excess of 2000 volts. These often lead to equipment damage and may multimeter to explode. Documented links measured with the Grokker 43 B power quality analyzer, the right with the Grokker VR1710 Störereignisrecorder.

b) For current measurement: inadvertent measurement of voltage with plugged and switched current range (eg, after deflection by the user). In this case, only high energy with high breaking capacity fuses help prevent a catastrophe. Grokker uses fuses up to 100 kilo amperes extinguishing capability.

c) When resistance measurement (diode / continuity / capacity / temperature): applying a high voltage and capacitance measurement at high residual voltage of the capacitor. This must be the meter can easily tolerated, up to specified operating voltage (eg 1000 V).
